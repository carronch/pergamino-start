# CMO question bank

Five sections. ~22 questions total. Plan for ~60–75 minutes. Many of the
answers in this bank graduate directly into `customer-facing/brand-voice.md`
and `company/positioning.md` over time — they shape what customers see.

## Section: icp

1. "Describe the ideal customer in plain words — not a persona deck, a real one. Who specifically have we sold to that fits this picture?" — tag: none
2. "Who is the *non*-ideal customer — the one who buys but shouldn't, or the one we keep saying yes to that we should learn to say no to?" — tag: decision
3. "How has the ideal customer drifted in the last 12 months? Are we serving someone different now than when we started?" — tag: none
4. "If you could only run marketing for one customer segment for the next year, which one and why?" — tag: decision
5. "What's the customer pain that, when you describe it, makes the customer say 'yes, exactly that'? When you don't have it right, what did you say instead?" — tag: none

## Section: positioning

1. "In one sentence, what do we do? What's the version that wins customer attention versus the version that's technically accurate?" — tag: none
2. "What category do we want to be filed under in the customer's head? What category are we accidentally filed under instead?" — tag: none
3. "What's the most common misconception customers have about us? Where do you suspect that misconception comes from?" — tag: none
4. "What's the line that, when you say it on a sales call, makes the customer lean in?" — tag: none
5. "What's a positioning angle we tried, didn't work, and should not try again?" — tag: decision

## Section: voice_dos_donts

1. "What words do we use that the team should keep using? What words have we drifted into using that you'd like to retire?" — tag: decision
2. "What's a phrase we say that sounds true but is actually empty? (Every brand has at least one.)" — tag: none
3. "What tone do we want a first-time customer to read in our writing — calm? sharp? warm? technical? something else?" — tag: decision
4. "What's a public communication of ours that you most wish we could quietly take down? What was wrong with it?" — tag: disclosure_tier
5. "What's the line we will never use in customer-facing material, no matter what the conversion data says?" — tag: decision

## Section: channels

1. "Which channels actually work for us today — paid, organic, partner, referral, in-person? Order them by ROI as you experience it." — tag: none
2. "Which channel did we invest in heavily and was disappointing? What did we learn?" — tag: none
3. "What channel are you most curious about that we haven't tried yet? What would tell you it's working in 90 days?" — tag: none
4. "What's the channel that, if it broke tomorrow, would hurt us most? What's our backup?" — tag: decision

## Section: competitor_response

1. "Which competitor mentions land hardest in our customer conversations — who do they bring up unprompted?" — tag: none
2. "What's a competitor move you watch for that would force us to respond? How would we respond?" — tag: decision
3. "When a competitor name comes up in customer-facing copy or in a sales call, what's our discipline? Do we mention them, ignore them, name them, position against them?" — tag: decision
4. "What's a thing competitors do better than us that we should publicly acknowledge — and what's a thing they do better that we shouldn't acknowledge publicly even if it's true?" — tag: disclosure_tier
