# Founder question bank

Five sections. ~22 questions total. Plan for ~60–90 minutes. This bank
overlaps with `ceo.md` when the founder still serves as CEO; run them on
separate days if both apply, and tell the leader so up front.

## Section: origin

1. "What were you doing the year before you started {{COMPANY_NAME}}? What was the thing you saw that nobody else seemed to take seriously?" — tag: none
2. "Who was the first person who told you the idea was worth pursuing? Who was the first person who told you it wouldn't work?" — tag: none
3. "What's the moment you committed — the moment you couldn't go back to the prior life? Date it if you can." — tag: none
4. "What was the version-zero of the product or service? How does what {{COMPANY_NAME}} does today differ from that?" — tag: none

## Section: founding_promises

1. "What did you promise yourself, the first year, that you would never do as the company grew? Are you still keeping any of those?" — tag: decision
2. "What did you promise customers — implicit or explicit — that becomes harder to keep as the company grows?" — tag: decision
3. "What did you promise an early employee or partner that the rest of the team may not know about? Is that promise still alive?" — tag: disclosure_tier
4. "What did you promise an investor or capital source that constrains what you can do today?" — tag: disclosure_tier

## Section: principles

1. "What's a rule the company runs by that, if a competitor saw it written down, you'd be fine — and what's a rule that you'd want to keep private?" — tag: disclosure_tier
2. "When you make a hard call, what is the first thing you weigh? Not the answer — the question you ask yourself first." — tag: decision
3. "What's a principle the company drifted away from, that you'd like back?" — tag: none
4. "What's a principle you used to hold that you've since dropped, and what changed your mind?" — tag: none

## Section: succession

1. "If you stepped away tomorrow for six months, who would you trust to make daily decisions? Has that person been told?" — tag: disclosure_tier
2. "Which decisions do you currently carry that should NOT pass to a successor — that would die with your departure?" — tag: decision
3. "What would have to be true for you to genuinely hand the company to someone else? What would you need to see in them?" — tag: decision
4. "What's a piece of institutional memory that lives in your head and nowhere else? Name as many as you can." — tag: none
5. "If your successor read everything in this brain in their first week, what would they STILL be missing about how to lead this company? How would they learn it?" — tag: none

## Section: what_kills_us

1. "What's the boring failure mode — the slow, unglamorous way the company could die over 24 months?" — tag: decision
2. "What's the dramatic failure mode — the single bad month or single bad call — that you most fear?" — tag: decision
3. "What's a competitor or new entrant that, if they made one specific move, would force you to seriously rethink the business?" — tag: none
4. "What's a regulatory, legal, or geopolitical event you watch for that would change everything?" — tag: none
5. "Honest read: what's the one thing about you, personally, that's most likely to be in the way of the company surviving the next decade?" — tag: disclosure_tier
