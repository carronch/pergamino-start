# Sales (head) question bank

Five sections. ~22 questions total. Plan for ~60–75 minutes. Many of these
answers will land in `sales/playbook.md` — be specific.

## Section: pipeline_stages

1. "Walk me through the pipeline as it actually exists today. Not the tool's stage names — the stages in your head." — tag: none
2. "Which stage has the highest drop-off? What kills deals there most often?" — tag: none
3. "Which stage is the longest? Is that good (real qualification) or bad (indecision masquerading as deliberation)?" — tag: none
4. "What signal tells you a deal is about to close — what does the customer say or do that you've learned to recognize?" — tag: none
5. "What signal tells you a deal is dead before the customer says it's dead? How early do you usually see it?" — tag: none

## Section: win_criteria

1. "Describe the last three wins. What did they have in common?" — tag: none
2. "What's the smallest number of customer conversations that have to happen before you'll commit a lot of effort? What's the trigger?" — tag: decision
3. "Who in the customer's organization needs to be on the phone for a deal to be real? When that person's not there, what does that mean?" — tag: decision
4. "What's the customer behavior that, looking back, was the strongest predictor of a long-term, healthy account — versus a one-time transaction?" — tag: none
5. "When you're competing against a known competitor and you win, what's typically the deciding factor in the customer's words?" — tag: none

## Section: lost_deal_rituals

1. "When you lose a deal, what do you do in the next 24 hours? Walk me through the actual ritual, not the aspirational one." — tag: none
2. "Have you ever changed pricing, packaging, or positioning because of one specific lost deal? What was it?" — tag: decision
3. "What's a class of customer that consistently churns or doesn't renew? Have we figured out why?" — tag: none
4. "When does a lost deal stay closed-lost, and when do you put it back in the pipeline 6–12 months later? What's the trigger?" — tag: decision
5. "What's a lost deal that haunts you — that you still think about? What does it tell you about the gap in our offer?" — tag: none

## Section: key_accounts

1. "Name our top accounts today. What does each represent — strategic, revenue, reference, all three?" — tag: disclosure_tier
2. "Which key account, if it left tomorrow, would hurt the most? Why?" — tag: disclosure_tier
3. "Which key account is most exposed — most likely to leave, even if they're paying today?" — tag: disclosure_tier
4. "What does a key account get that a normal account doesn't? Pricing? Access? Custom work? Where's the line, and who decides when to cross it?" — tag: decision
5. "Which key account taught us the most — about ourselves, our offer, our limits?" — tag: none

## Section: pricing_flexibility

1. "What's the public price by tier, and what's the actual range you've sold at? How wide is the gap?" — tag: disclosure_tier
2. "When can a salesperson discount on their own authority? When does it have to escalate, and to whom?" — tag: decision
3. "What's a discount or concession that, in retrospect, you wish we hadn't offered? What did we learn?" — tag: none
4. "Have you ever raised prices on existing customers? What was the trigger, what was the process, what was the result?" — tag: decision
5. "What's the price point at which you most often see customers say no — the elasticity wall, in our category?" — tag: none
