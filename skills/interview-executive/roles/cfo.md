# CFO question bank

Six sections. ~28 questions total. Plan for ~60–90 minutes. The
disclosure_tiers section feeds directly into `policies/{person-slug}-disclosure.md`,
which the customer-facing reviewer-agent consults — answer it carefully.

## Section: revenue_lines

1. "What are the revenue lines today, in plain language? Don't read me the chart of accounts — describe them like you'd describe them to a new employee." — tag: none
2. "Which line is the cleanest — most predictable, lowest variance? Which line is the messiest?" — tag: none
3. "Which revenue line do you most want to grow this year? Which one are you quietly trying to sunset?" — tag: decision
4. "What revenue line, if it disappeared next month, would matter least? Which would matter most?" — tag: decision
5. "What revenue line do you NOT yet have but think is the natural next thing? What would it require?" — tag: none

## Section: cost_structure

1. "Walk me through the top 5 cost categories. What percentage of total spend does each represent?" — tag: none
2. "Which cost line is most likely to surprise you in the next 12 months — most likely to spike?" — tag: decision
3. "Which cost is locked in and you can't easily change? Which is the most flexible — you could cut it tomorrow?" — tag: decision
4. "What's the expense the team thinks is essential but you secretly suspect is optional?" — tag: disclosure_tier
5. "Have we ever cut a cost we later regretted? What did we learn?" — tag: none

## Section: cash_discipline

1. "What does the cash position look like today? Months of runway, ideally — or whatever measure you use." — tag: disclosure_tier
2. "What's the cash-management ritual — daily, weekly, monthly? Who else watches it with you?" — tag: none
3. "Under what conditions would you raise outside capital? Under what conditions would you absolutely refuse to?" — tag: decision
4. "What's the ratio between cash-on-hand and your most volatile expense? Is that ratio comfortable, tight, or scary right now?" — tag: disclosure_tier
5. "When was the last time the company had a genuinely close call on cash? What pulled it back?" — tag: disclosure_tier

## Section: banking

1. "Which banks does the company use? What's the role of each — operating, savings, FX, payroll?" — tag: disclosure_tier
2. "Are there any banking relationships you keep deliberately private from the broader team? Why?" — tag: disclosure_tier
3. "What are the signing thresholds — who can move how much money, with whose co-signature?" — tag: decision
4. "Have you ever had a banking relationship fail or freeze? What did you learn about redundancy?" — tag: none

## Section: reporting_cadence

1. "Who sees the financials, and at what cadence? Be specific: which file, which numbers, every how often, by what mechanism?" — tag: decision
2. "Which numbers do you watch every day, even if nobody else does? Why those?" — tag: none
3. "Which numbers do you only look at quarterly or annually? Why is that the right cadence for them?" — tag: none
4. "What's the report format you most rely on? What's a report format you abandoned because it didn't survive contact with reality?" — tag: none

## Section: disclosure_tiers

(any employee), **internal-leadership** (department heads + above),
**board-only**, **public** (customer-facing OK).

4. "Cash position / runway — what tier?" — tag: disclosure_tier
5. "Compensation ranges by role (anonymized) — what tier?" — tag: disclosure_tier
