# CEO question bank

Six sections. ~25 questions total. Plan for ~60–90 minutes; the leader
should be uninterrupted.

## Section: vision

1. "What is {{COMPANY_NAME}} actually trying to be in three years? Not the slogan — the picture in your head." — tag: none
2. "What are you most worried about getting wrong on the way there?" — tag: none
3. "When you imagine the company at that future state, what's the one thing about today that you'd most like to be able to forget?" — tag: none
4. "What's the version of {{COMPANY_NAME}} you would be embarrassed to become, even if it made money?" — tag: none

## Section: priorities

1. "What are the three things that, if they break this quarter, you stop everything else to fix?" — tag: decision
2. "What's the one project right now where you genuinely don't know if it's going to work?" — tag: none
3. "What did you say yes to in the last 60 days that, looking back, you wish you'd said no to? Why did you say yes?" — tag: none
4. "What is the cheapest experiment we haven't run that you most want to know the result of?" — tag: none

## Section: decision_rights

1. "Walk me through a recent decision where you should have delegated and didn't. What kept you in the chair?" — tag: decision
2. "What's a class of decision you'd like to fully delegate over the next year? What would have to be true for that?" — tag: decision
3. "When a department head and you disagree on something operational, what's your tiebreak? An example, ideally." — tag: decision
4. "Which decisions in the last year did you actively want to make personally, and would not delegate even if you could?" — tag: decision

## Section: escalation

1. "What kinds of issues should reach you within the same day, no matter what?" — tag: decision
2. "What kinds of issues should reach you weekly, in a bundle?" — tag: decision
3. "What kinds of issues should never reach you, and you'd be annoyed if they did?" — tag: decision
4. "Which person on the team is most likely to escalate something they shouldn't have? Which is most likely to NOT escalate something they should have?" — tag: none

## Section: relationships

1. "Who outside the company are the three people whose opinions you most care about right now? What do they push you on?" — tag: none
2. "Inside the company, who do you think is most underutilized? Who is most overweight on responsibility?" — tag: none
3. "What's a relationship — investor, customer, partner — that you're consciously managing more carefully than the others? Why?" — tag: none
4. "When was the last time you said no to a relationship that someone on the team thought you should say yes to?" — tag: none

## Section: never_say_publicly

1. "What's something true about the business that you would never put in writing to a customer?" — tag: disclosure_tier
2. "What's something true about a competitor that you would never say in public, even though it's true?" — tag: disclosure_tier
3. "What internal financial truth — a margin, a dependency, a runway figure — do you NOT want surfaced in casual conversation, even with the team?" — tag: disclosure_tier
4. "If a journalist sat in our weekly leadership meeting, what's the one moment in the last quarter you'd want them to NOT have heard?" — tag: disclosure_tier
5. "Which person, if they left, would you most quietly hustle to retain — and you'd want that fact to stay between us?" — tag: disclosure_tier
